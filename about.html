Bio

Lindsey McKim is a senior Dance and Media Arts and Design double major with a concentration in Interactive Design at James Madison University.

Lindsey is a member of the Virginia Repertory Dance Company, JMU's pre-professional dance company. Throughout her time at JMU, Lindsey has worked with Kyle Abraham, Gregory Dolbashian, Kiki Lucas, DanceWorks Chicago, Stephanie Liapis, Jess Burgess, Emily Wright, and JMU Dance Faculty. During her sophomore year, Lindsey attended the Mid-Atlantic South American College Dance Association conference at Meredith College where she had the opportunity to perform and take classes. Lindsey has performed in six student dance concerts and five mainstage dance concerts. She has had choreography selected to be performed in two student dance concerts at JMU. In the summers, Lindsey has attended dance programs with the School of Toronto Dance Theatre and Christoper K. Morgan and Artists. Lindsey currently serves as the Student Dubbing/Media position for the School of Theatre and Dance at JMU, a member of the VRDC and Contemporary Dance Ensemble, and most recently had her work performed in a virtual student dance concert.

Throughout her time in her SMAD major, Lindsey has created an immense amount of design and UX projects. Last spring, her professor entered her and her partner's work into the AEJMC's Best of the Web competition for their app "PD Connect." She also started her freelance web design career this summer after designing a site for Downtown Studios Unlimited in Luray.
